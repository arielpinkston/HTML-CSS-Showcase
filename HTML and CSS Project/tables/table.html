My Art Journey

One of my greatest passions is creating art. I love the feeling of control it gives me. I get to decide how the finished product is going to look. In addition to that, I love that this is a hobby I can progress in throughout my life. I started my art journey a few years ago, and I have experienced monumental improvement since then. To say the least, I'm excited to see how my talents in art will develop in the years to come.

The Beginning

When I first started drawing and creating art, I began by sketching with simple pencil and paper. Soon after this, I discovered As time went on and I got better with that, I worked up the courage the social media platforms of other artists. Seeing the work of these artists inspired me to keep going when I wanted to give up. Their tips helped me to improve my own methods. Most of the artists I admired worked in digital art, and this was one of the main factors that encouraged me to dabble in digital art. In my senior year of high school, I took a painting class that completely changed my outlook on art. Without that class, I would have never discovered my passion for the art medium of acrylic paint and canvas. In retrospect, I can say with confidence that my experience in digital art was the foundation for my talent for painting with acrylics.

Today

Recently, I have been trying to go back to what I started with: sketching with pencil and paper. I decided to go back to this method because I want to primarily focus on building a good foundation. I try my best to sketch for a few minutes every day, and while I can't always make this happen, the repetition of this method has helped me improve greatly. Before I started doing this, I would agonize over every line and curve. Now, I have enough confidence to let my mind go and just draw. I can simply focus on my reference picture and let my hand copy it. This was another thing that following art influencers helped me with. One of the most popular topics that art influencers stress is practicing regularly because that will help one gain confidence in drawing. Not only that, I have also learned that you must always use a reference picture, especially if you're a beginner at drawing. While some believe using a reference picture is “cheating,” I firmly believe that using reference pictures is how an artist learns how to draw in the first place. Above all else, I believe this is what has helped me the most. By using and copying reference pictures, I have learned what objects actually look like.

My Passion

My favorite thing to draw is people. There are an infinite number of positions and expressions a person can appear in. While this fact could be overwhelming to some artists, this is what attracts me to my passion for drawing people. More specifically, I love being able to capture emotion. I love identifying the facets of each line and how those make up the person I am drawing. I love taking inspiration from the uniqueness of the people in my reference pictures and summarizing them in my drawings.
